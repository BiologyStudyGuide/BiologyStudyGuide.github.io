Characterisivs of Life

NOTES:

Carbs: 1:2:1 ratio (C:H:O) (monosaccharide & polysaccharide) also table sugar C12H22O11 is also a carb, but it does not follow the rule. Can be broken down for chemical energy. Short term storage. Monomer – monosaccharides. Polymer – polysaccharide.

Lipids: Glycerol head and fatty acid tails (fatty acids and triglycerides). It is long term storage and stores more than carbohydrates. Polymer – triglyceride. Special type: phospholipid.

Proteins: Carbon center connected to a hydrogen, carboxyl group (COOH), amino group(H2N), and R group (Amino acids and polypeptides). It has a lot of functions (enzyme, transport, signaling, etc.)

Nucleic Acids: Phosphate group, carbon ring, nitrogen base (DNA, RNA, ATP) (Nucleotides)

Top left: Carbohydrate

Top right: Lipids

Bottom left: Proteins

Bottom right: Nucleic Acid

Enzymes lower the activation energy needed for the reaction. They are proteins so they can denature if the pH and temperature are changed.

Enzymes work 2 ways, the lock and key and the Induced fit.

The lock and key method are when the substrate fits exactly in the enzyme activity zone, and it was the method that scientist's thought was the only one for many years.

Induced fit is the theory where the substrate changes to fit into the enzyme where it can bond. It was suggested by Daniel Koshland in 1958.

Water stuff:

• cohesive behavior – Water molecules stick together.

• ability to moderate temperature - Water has a high specific heat

• expansion upon freezing - Water decreases in density after freezing

• versatility as a solvent – Water is known as the universal solvent due to its polarity.

• hydrogen bonding and polarity – Hydrogen bonds made between the hydrogen and the oxygen atoms in H2O the oxygen is slightly negative, and the hydrogens are slightly positive
